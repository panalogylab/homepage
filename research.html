Social AI Alignment

We are at the beginning of an age where AI systems operate as long-lived agents interacting with humans and other artifacts in real-world complex social environments. Aligning these social AI systems, when they are operating in societies, with evolving human intentions and values is a critical challenge whose complexity we still know little about. At Panalogy Lab, we take an approach to alignment research built upon an interdisciplinary understanding of emergent behaviors of social AI systems within the complex social context in which they operate. Our research focuses on three main themes:

1. Complex decision-making capability: We study behaviors of AI systems that exhibits capability to make complex social decisions. Currently, our focus of attention is on studying augmented language models using a mixture of prompt engineering and reinforcement learning

2. Behavioral complexity: We seek to study behavioral complexity of AI systems in evolving complex social context rather than rigidly managed and simplified environments. Currently, we are investigating social settings that are pivotal in human societies.

3. Training AI systems to do research: Our long-term goal is to be able to train AI systems that can effectively do complex social alignment research using our own insights into behavioral complexity. We pursue this approach because we believe social AI systems will soon operate in environments whose complexity will be beyond our capability to understand directly. It’s best to focus our effort on designing AI systems and evaluating their performance while delegating the cognitive load of doing research to these AI systems.

Please contact us for further information and discussions on research projects we are working on.


Social AI Design

What would be a satisfactory design for an AI system that operates in a complex social environment? We structure the design process using the following insights from our research.

Social decision-making: We focus on designing systems that must ultimately make complex decisions in evolving social context. These decisions are the driving force behind emergent behavioral complexity that’s the subject of our social AI alignment research. We believe that complex decision-making is an important cognitive hurdle that between narrow AI and AGI, and a focus on this aspect will bring us a step closer to making contribution to the development of AGI.

Behavioral uncertainty: As social environments in which an AI system operates become more complex, behavioral uncertainty will be a constant factor that our design must take into account. Evolving social context and emergent behaviors will eventually lead to situations never expected before. Dealing with this challenge is essentially a task of generalizing decision-making capability to deal with behavioral uncertainty.

Our current design ideas:

1. "her": a voice-based conversational AI agent built upon SOTA LLMs, speech synthesis AI, prompt engineering, and reinforcement learning. We designed "her" with the goal of stuyding what social behaviors make a conversational AI agent "natural" from a human perspective

2. AI agents in realistic social settings: reinforcement learning models designed to operate in realistic social environments such as financial markets, dating, and sport. One important aspect of these models is the use of LLMs as part of their learning process.

3. Expert recommender: this project designs augmented language models that enable organisations to build optimal expert teams who meet complex criteria for large-scale projects in matter of hours instead of weeks at a cost never seen before. The project is a partnership between BEST at QUT and Panalogy Lab.

4. Computational history of intellect and policy in science (CHIPS): CHIPS is a built upon a set of augmented language models with a single purpose in mind: to enable organisations to build optimal expert teams who meet complex criteria for large-scale projects in matter of hours instead of weeks at a cost never seen before. The project is a partnership between BEST and QUT and Panalogy Lab
